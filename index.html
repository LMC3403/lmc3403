Overview

Poetry can be a powerful instructional tool or simply a source of enjoyment and can be entertaining to all ages. The Poetry Vending Machine is used to distribute poems, specifically on university campuses, engaging student-aged audiences in poetry. The Poetry Vending Machine allows its owner to distribute poems of their choosing. The owner has the ability to group poems into three categories (e.g. by author, by topic, by style, etc). The purpose of the machine is to:

  1. give users an opportunity to explore many genres and poets.
  2. allow users to share their own poetry with others.
  3. stimulate conversations about poetry.
The machine should be placed in an open area with plenty of foot traffic (e.g. a hallway in a university building or near an office water fountain). Passersby should be able to clearly see the machine and be able to approach it easily to quickly have a poem dispensed to them.

Description of Equipment

       




The Poetry Vending Machine is modeled after a snack vending machine. Paper strips with poems on them will be folded and placed between loops in coils. There are three knobs, one for each coil, that the user can turn to rotate the coil and push a poem forward, causing it to fall into a collection tray. This style machine allows the machine’s owner to reload poems and separate them into categories of their choosing.

Troubleshooting

  • No poems are being dispensed when the knob is turned.
    1. With the machine upright, remove the back of the machine.
    2. If the machine needs to be reloaded, simply follow steps 1-5 in Loading the Machine.
    3. If a poem is snagged in the coil or on the platform, remove it, re-fold it, and place it in a gap of the coils such that the bottom of the paper is resting on the bottom of the platform below.
  • The knob won’t rotate.
    1. With the machine upright, remove the back of the machine.
    2. Check that no poems are snagged in the coils.
    3. If a poem is snagged, remove it, re-fold it, and place it in a gap of the coils such that the bottom of the paper is resting on the bottom of the platform below.
  • I cannot remove the back of my Poetry Vending Machine.
    1. Please contact the manufacturer.
  • I’m missing a part of my Poetry Vending Machine.
    1. Please contact the manufacturer for information on obtaining replacement parts.
