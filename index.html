UPS Inc.

Software Engineer Intern • May 2016 - August 2016

My project was to simulate the UPS Ground Freight Network to better understand terminal capacity and provide a platform for scenario analysis. Thus, the model will produce results that represent how proposed UPGF Operational changes affected the overall network.
The model can flow and process shipments as they move through the UPGF network. Shipments are randomly generated and fed into the model and will aggregate all incoming shipment volume by location and assign a “next terminal” to each shipment based on available lane volume and probabilistic splits for alt-flowed shipments. Shipments in transit will be delayed the appropriate number of days for time in transit. Late and mis-flowed shipments will be taken into account probabilistically and delayed accordingly. The model tracked the following:
   - Capacity Per Lane
   - Mileage
   - Travel time
   - Costs per Shipment
My Arena model project was finished and was successfully able to model the entire UPGF Network and produced results that stated there were possible saving opportunities of up to a $1,000,000.
